# Understanding and Preventing RHDV2 in Rabbits

Rabbit Hemorrhagic Disease Virus 2 (RHDV2) is a highly contagious and often fatal virus that affects both domestic and wild rabbits. It is a newer, more aggressive strain of Rabbit Hemorrhagic Disease Virus (RHDV) that has been spreading across North America. Understanding this disease and taking preventative measures, including vaccination, is crucial for every rabbit owner. [1]

## What is RHDV2?

RHDV2 is a calicivirus that causes a severe and rapid-onset disease in rabbits. Unlike earlier strains, RHDV2 can affect rabbits of all ages and has a mortality rate of 70-90%. [2] The virus is incredibly resilient and can survive in the environment for months, making it easy to spread.

**Transmission:**

RHDV2 is extremely hardy and can be spread through numerous direct and indirect routes:

*   **Direct contact** with an infected rabbit (alive or dead).
*   **Contact with contaminated objects**, such as clothing, shoes, cages, food and water bowls, and bedding.
*   **Contact with the urine or feces** of an infected rabbit.
*   **Insect vectors** like flies and fleas can carry the virus.
*   **People** can unknowingly carry the virus on their hands, clothing, and shoes after being in a contaminated area. [3]

> RHDV2 does not affect humans or other animal species, but they can act as carriers and spread the virus to rabbits. [4]

## The Importance of Vaccination

Vaccination is the most effective tool available to protect your rabbit from RHDV2. Due to the highly contagious and fatal nature of the virus, all pet rabbits should be vaccinated, even if they live indoors. [5]

In the United States, the primary vaccine available is produced by Medgene Labs. This is a killed, recombinant vaccine that has been proven safe and effective in providing lasting immunity against RHDV2. [6]

**Vaccination Schedule:**

The typical vaccination protocol for the Medgene RHDV2 vaccine is as follows:

| Vaccination | Age | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Initial Dose** | Can be given as early as 4 weeks of age. | The first of a two-dose series. |
| **Booster Dose** | 21 days after the initial dose. | Essential for achieving full immunity. |
| **Annual Booster** | Every 12 months. | Maintains a protective level of immunity. |

Full protection is typically achieved about 14 days after the second (booster) dose. [7]

## Biosecurity: Your Second Line of Defense

While vaccination is critical, practicing good biosecurity is also essential to minimize the risk of exposure.

*   **Wash Hands**: Always wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water before and after handling your rabbit.
*   **Dedicated Clothing**: If you are in contact with other rabbits or in areas with wild rabbit populations, have dedicated shoes and clothing that you only wear around your own rabbits.
*   **Quarantine New Animals**: Any new rabbit should be quarantined for at least 14-30 days before being introduced to your existing rabbits.
*   **Control Insects**: Use rabbit-safe flea and fly control methods to reduce the risk of insect-borne transmission.
*   **Avoid Foraging**: Do not feed your rabbit grass, weeds, or branches collected from outdoors, as these could be contaminated by wild rabbits. [3]

## Is the Vaccine Safe?

The Medgene RHDV2 vaccine is a killed vaccine, meaning it does not contain any live virus and cannot cause the disease. It has an excellent safety profile. The most common side effects are mild and temporary, such as a small lump at the injection site, mild lethargy, or a temporary decrease in appetite. Serious adverse reactions are very rare. [6]

## How to Get Your Rabbit Vaccinated

The RHDV2 vaccine must be administered by a licensed veterinarian. Not all clinics carry the vaccine, so you may need to call around to find an exotic animal vet in your area that offers it. [8]

Protecting your rabbit from RHDV2 is a critical part of responsible pet ownership. Talk to your veterinarian about getting your rabbit vaccinated as soon as possible.
