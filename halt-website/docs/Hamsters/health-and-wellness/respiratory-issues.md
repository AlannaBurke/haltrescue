# Respiratory Issues in Hamsters

Respiratory infections can be a serious concern for hamsters. Their small size means that a simple upper respiratory infection (a cold) can quickly progress to pneumonia, which is often life-threatening. These infections are frequently linked to the hamster's environment, making proper husbandry the most critical tool for prevention.

## Causes of Respiratory Infections

Respiratory infections in hamsters are typically bacterial. The infection often takes hold when the hamster's respiratory system is weakened or irritated by environmental factors.

*   **Improper Bedding:** This is a leading cause. Aromatic beddings like cedar and non-kiln-dried pine shavings release toxic oils (phenols) that severely damage the sensitive lining of the respiratory tract. Overly dusty beddings can also cause irritation.
*   **Ammonia Buildup:** A dirty, poorly ventilated cage will have high levels of ammonia from urine. These fumes are highly irritating to the lungs and make them susceptible to infection.
*   **Drafts and Temperature Changes:** Sudden chills or being housed in a drafty location can stress a hamster's system and make them more vulnerable to illness.
*   **Stress:** Other stressors, such as a barren cage, loud noises, or overcrowding, can weaken the immune system.

## Signs of a Respiratory Problem

A healthy hamster is bright and active in the evening. A sick hamster will often be lethargic and show clear signs of illness.

*   **Sneezing, Wheezing, or Coughing.**
*   **Clicking or Rattling Sounds:** A clicking sound when the hamster breathes is a classic sign of a respiratory infection in small rodents.
*   **Labored Breathing:** The hamster's sides may heave with the effort of breathing.
*   **Nasal or Eye Discharge.**
*   **Loss of Appetite.**
*   **Lethargy:** The hamster may not wake up at its usual time, may not be interested in playing or running on its wheel, and may huddle in a corner.

## Diagnosis and Treatment

**Any hamster showing signs of respiratory distress should see a veterinarian as soon as possible.**

*   **Diagnosis:** The vet will listen to the hamster's chest with a stethoscope to assess the severity of the infection. A chest x-ray may be needed to diagnose pneumonia.
*   **Treatment:** A course of oral antibiotics prescribed by the veterinarian is the standard treatment. It is vital to complete the full course of medication. In severe cases, supportive care, such as providing extra warmth and ensuring the hamster stays hydrated, is also important.

## Prevention: A Safe and Clean Habitat

Good husbandry is the best defense against respiratory infections.

*   **Use Safe Bedding:** Choose paper-based beddings, aspen shavings, or kiln-dried pine. **Never use cedar or non-kiln-dried pine.**
*   **Ensure Good Ventilation:** The cage should have good airflow to prevent the buildup of ammonia. If using a tank, it must have a wire mesh lid.
*   **Keep the Cage Clean:** Spot clean daily and perform full bedding changes regularly.
*   **Avoid Drafts:** Place the cage in a location free from drafts and sudden temperature fluctuations.
*   **Provide a Proper Diet:** A balanced diet supports a strong immune system.
