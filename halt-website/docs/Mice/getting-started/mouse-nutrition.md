# Mouse Nutrition

A proper diet is crucial for the health and well-being of pet mice. As omnivores, they require a balanced mix of grains, seeds, and protein. Providing the right food will help prevent obesity and ensure they get all the necessary nutrients.

## Staple Diet: Lab Blocks are Key

The best foundation for a mouse's diet is a high-quality commercial lab block or pellet.

*   **Recommended:** **Lab blocks** (like Mazuri Rat & Mouse or Oxbow Essentials Mouse & Young Rat) are the ideal choice. They are nutritionally complete and prevent selective feeding, ensuring your mouse gets a balanced diet in every bite.
*   **Acceptable:** A high-quality seed mix can be used, but you must ensure your mice are eating all of it, not just the fatty seeds. Supplementing a seed mix with lab blocks is a good strategy.

Because of their high metabolism, mice should have access to their staple food at all times.

## Safe Fresh Foods

Offer a small, pea-sized amount of fresh food 2-3 times per week to supplement their diet.

| Category | Safe Foods (in small amounts) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Vegetables** | Broccoli, Carrot, Kale, Peas, Cucumber, Bell Pepper |
| **Fruits** | Apple (no seeds), Banana, Pear, Berries |
| **Protein** | Cooked plain chicken or beef, scrambled egg, mealworms |
| **Grains** | Cooked pasta or rice, oats |

## Foods to Avoid

Many foods are harmful to mice. Never feed them:

*   Grapes or Raisins
*   Onion or Garlic
*   Citrus Fruits
*   Chocolate or Caffeine
*   Avocado
*   Walnuts
*   Rhubarb
*   Any junk food, sugary, or salty snacks

## Water

Fresh, clean water must always be available from a sipper bottle. Check the bottle daily to ensure it is full and not clogged.
