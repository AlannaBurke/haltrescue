# Common Health Issues in Guinea Pigs

Guinea pigs are wonderful pets, but they have specific health needs and are prone to certain problems. As prey animals, they hide illness well, so it's vital for owners to be familiar with the subtle signs of these common conditions.

## 1. Scurvy (Vitamin C Deficiency)

Like humans, guinea pigs cannot produce their own Vitamin C. A lack of this essential vitamin in their diet leads to scurvy, a painful and potentially fatal condition.

*   **Signs:** Lethargy, weakness, reluctance to move, swollen and painful joints, poor coat quality, and a decreased appetite.
*   **Prevention:** Provide a daily, stable source of Vitamin C. This can be through high-quality guinea pig pellets fortified with Vitamin C, a small portion of a low-calcium, high-Vitamin C vegetable like bell pepper, or a plain Vitamin C supplement (tablet or liquid). Note that Vitamin C degrades quickly, especially in water bottles exposed to light.

## 2. Gastrointestinal (GI) Stasis

This is a common and life-threatening emergency where the digestive system slows down or stops completely. It can be triggered by pain, stress, dehydration, or an improper diet.

*   **Signs:** **This is a critical emergency.** Signs include loss of appetite, small or no fecal pellets, lethargy, and a hunched posture indicating pain.
*   **Action:** If you notice these signs, contact a guinea pig-savvy veterinarian immediately. Treatment involves pain management, hydration, and medications to stimulate gut motility.
*   **Prevention:** The diet must consist of at least 80% high-quality grass hay (like timothy or orchard). Hay provides the essential fiber to keep the gut moving.
*   **More Info:** [GI Health in Guinea Pigs](./gi-health)

## 3. Bladder Stones (Uroliths)

Guinea pigs are prone to forming painful mineral stones in their bladder. This is often linked to a diet too high in calcium.

*   **Signs:** Squeaking or crying during urination, blood in the urine, frequent urination with little output, and lethargy.
*   **Management:** This is a very painful condition that requires veterinary diagnosis and often surgery to remove the stones. A diet lower in calcium can help prevent recurrence.

## 4. Mites

Mange mites are microscopic parasites that burrow under the skin, causing intense itching and pain. The pain can be so severe that it can trigger seizures.

*   **Signs:** Extreme scratching, hair loss, flaky skin, and signs of pain when touched.
*   **Action:** **This is a veterinary emergency.** Do not attempt to treat with over-the-counter pet store remedies. A vet will prescribe a safe and effective anti-parasitic medication.

## 5. Respiratory Infections

Guinea pigs are susceptible to respiratory infections, which can quickly become serious pneumonia.

*   **Signs:** Sneezing, wheezing, nasal discharge, watery eyes, labored breathing, and loss of appetite.
*   **Prevention:** Keep the cage clean and dry, use dust-free bedding, and house them in a draft-free but well-ventilated area.
*   **More Info:** [Respiratory Issues in Guinea Pigs](./respiratory-issues)
