# Grooming Your Pet Hedgehog

Grooming is an important part of hedgehog care, not just for cleanliness but also as an opportunity to check their health and strengthen your bond. The two main grooming tasks for a hedgehog are bathing and nail trimming.

## Bathing Your Hedgehog

Hedgehogs are prone to getting messy feet from running on their wheel all night, a condition affectionately known as "poop boots." While they don't need frequent full-body baths, occasional foot baths are a necessity. [1]

*   **Frequency**: Full baths should be infrequent, perhaps once a month or only as needed. Over-bathing can dry out their sensitive skin. Foot baths can be done as often as needed.
*   **The Process**:
    1.  Fill a sink or basin with about 1-2 inches of warm (not hot) water.
    2.  Place your hedgehog in the water. Most will uncurl once their feet touch the bottom.
    3.  Use a soft toothbrush or a cloth to gently scrub their feet and quills. If using a soap, it must be a gentle, fragrance-free option like an oatmeal-based baby shampoo. [2]
    4.  Rinse them thoroughly with clean, warm water, being very careful to avoid getting water in their ears.
    5.  Towel dry them completely and ensure they are in a warm, draft-free environment until they are fully dry.

## Nail Trimming

A hedgehog's nails grow constantly and need to be trimmed every few weeks to prevent them from curling and causing discomfort. [3]

*   **The Best Time**: The easiest time to trim their nails is during or right after a bath. The warm water helps to soften the nails, and many hedgehogs are more relaxed and less likely to curl into a ball. [4]
*   **The Tools**: A pair of small human nail clippers or cat claw scissors works well.
*   **The Technique**: Gently take one foot and trim just the very tip of the nail. Avoid the pink part, called the quick, which will bleed if cut. If you do accidentally cause bleeding, a bit of cornstarch or styptic powder can be used to stop it.

Be patient. It may take a few sessions for your hedgehog to get used to the process. Regular, gentle grooming will keep your spiky friend clean, comfortable, and healthy.
