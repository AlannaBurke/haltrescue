# Common Health Issues in Guinea Pigs

Guinea pigs are beloved for their gentle nature and vocal personalities, but they come with a unique set of health considerations. Many of their most common ailments are directly tied to their specific dietary needs and sensitive body systems. A knowledgeable and observant owner is the best defense against these common problems.

## 1. Vitamin C Deficiency (Scurvy)

Like humans, guinea pigs cannot produce their own Vitamin C. Without a consistent daily source in their diet, they will develop scurvy, a potentially fatal condition [1].

*   **Signs:** Lethargy, weakness, difficulty walking, swollen joints, loss of appetite, poor coat condition.
*   **Prevention:** This is 100% preventable. Provide a daily source of Vitamin C through high-quality guinea pig pellets fortified with Vitamin C, and fresh vegetables high in Vitamin C (like bell peppers and leafy greens). Many owners also supplement with liquid Vitamin C (25-30 mg/kg daily).

## 2. Gastrointestinal (GI) Stasis and Bloat

The guinea pig digestive system is designed to be in constant motion. If they stop eating for any reason (due to pain, stress, or illness), the gut can slow down or stop completely (GI stasis), which is a life-threatening emergency. Bloat (a buildup of gas) is also a critical emergency.

*   **Signs:** Loss of appetite, small or no fecal pellets, lethargy, hunched posture, and a hard or swollen abdomen.
*   **Prevention:** A diet high in fiber from unlimited grass hay is essential to keep the gut moving. Avoid sudden dietary changes and monitor your guinea pig's appetite and droppings daily. Any time a guinea pig stops eating, it is an emergency.
*   **More Info:** [GI Health in Guinea Pigs](./gi-health)

## 3. Bladder Stones and Sludge

Guinea pigs are prone to developing bladder stones (uroliths) or a thick, gritty sediment known as bladder sludge. This is often related to diet and genetics.

*   **Signs:** Squeaking or crying during urination, blood in the urine, frequent urination with little output, and a wet bottom.
*   **Management:** This condition requires veterinary diagnosis and can be very painful. Treatment may involve surgery to remove stones and dietary changes to reduce calcium intake. Limiting high-calcium foods like alfalfa hay (for adults) and some vegetables can help in prevention [2].

## 4. Bumblefoot (Pododermatitis)

This is a painful infection of the footpads, often caused by wire-bottom cages, abrasive bedding, obesity, or unsanitary conditions. It can lead to severe pain, abscesses, and bone infections.

*   **Signs:** Swollen, red, or ulcerated footpads; lameness; reluctance to move.
*   **Prevention:** House guinea pigs on soft, clean, dry bedding like fleece or aspen. Avoid wire floors. Keep the cage meticulously clean and help your guinea pig maintain a healthy weight.

## 5. Parasites (Mites and Lice)

Guinea pigs are very susceptible to skin parasites, particularly mites. Mange mites (Trixacarus caviae) burrow under the skin and cause intense itching, hair loss, and can even lead to seizures from the severe discomfort.

*   **Signs:** Intense scratching, hair loss, crusty skin, and sometimes frantic running or seizures.
*   **Treatment:** This is a treatable but serious condition that requires a veterinarian to prescribe an appropriate anti-parasitic medication, such as Ivermectin or Selamectin.
*   **More Info:** [Parasites in Guinea Pigs](./parasites)

## 6. Respiratory Infections

Guinea pigs are susceptible to pneumonia and other respiratory infections, often caused by bacteria like Bordetella.

*   **Signs:** Labored breathing, sneezing, nasal discharge, watery eyes, loss of appetite.
*   **Prevention:** Keep them in a draft-free, well-ventilated area. Use dust-free bedding. Importantly, **never house guinea pigs and rabbits together**, as rabbits can be asymptomatic carriers of Bordetella, which is often fatal to guinea pigs.
*   **More Info:** [Respiratory Issues in Guinea Pigs](./respiratory-issues)

Daily health checks and a deep understanding of these common issues are essential for any guinea pig owner.
